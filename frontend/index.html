Timespace NYC

This map distorts New York City so that distances represent transit times instead of geographic distance.

Neighborhoods well-connected by subway appear closer together, while poorly-served areas drift further apart.

How it works

  1. Collect transit times between 139 NYC neighborhoods
  2. Embed the transit-time matrix into 2D using multidimensional scaling (MDS)
  3. Build a Delaunay triangulation in geographic space
  4. Compute piecewise affine transforms from geographic to timespace coordinates
  5. Apply the distortion to all map features

What to look for

  • Manhattan compresses — most of it is within 20 minutes by subway
  • Express subway corridors pull distant neighborhoods closer
  • Areas without subway access (eastern Queens, southern Brooklyn, Staten Island) expand dramatically
